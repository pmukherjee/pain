Acute vs. Chronic

Pain can be acute (shore lived) or chronic (long lasting). Most pain is acute - like pain you'd get if you broke your arn. Acute pain is a signal of an injury and the need to protect yourself by not using the arm unit until it has healed. When pain lasts more than 3 months, it is chronic. Chronic pain is not an alert of more injury, even though you are still hurting. It is like a broken car horn; you still hear the sound even though there is no danger. Stopping activity is not helpful. In fact, not moving enough cause your muscles to lose strength and become tight and sore, causing more pain. There are other ways the treatment of chronic and acute pain are different. When pain is acute, treatment may include rest and medication and possibly surgery.
